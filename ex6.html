Goal: Find the exact constant annual deposit (D) needed to finance your financial freedom.

üìå Context: The Two Phases of Life

Imagine a timeline clearly divided into two distinct parts:

  • Phase 1 (Saving): Years 0 to 4 (5 deposits). You work and save money.
  • Phase 2 (Retirement): Years 5 to 14 (10 withdrawals). You stop working and live off your reserves.

The Mission: Find the exact deposit amount (D) so that your account balances to exactly 0 after the very last withdrawal.

"At year 5, how much money do I need to have?"

üí° The Intuition:

We want to know the exact present value that generates 10 withdrawals of 30k. We will use the variable C5 for this result. This is the price of your freedom on the exact first day of your retirement.

STEP 2 ‚Äî The Value of this Need Today (Year 0)

"What does this huge amount represent in today's money?"
C5 is located in the future. To compare it with your efforts today (your deposits), you must bring it back to Year 0. We "deflate" it by stripping away 5 years of interest.

The Formula:
1. General Mathematical Structure:

2. Applied Version:

üí° The Intuition:
