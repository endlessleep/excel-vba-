Definition: m is the Number of compounding periods per year (the number of times interest is added to the capital in a single year).

The Division Rule

We never keep the annual rate as is. The Interest per compounding period must be calculated as: r / m.

How much is $1 deposited every year for 5 years worth today?

=PV(r ; 5 ; -1 ;; 1)
