EX 2: TWO-STAGE GORDON MODEL

üí° The Core Principle of Share Valuation

A share is worth the present value of all future dividends. Price today reflects future cash flows.

P₀ = ∑ [ Dt / (1+r)ᵃ ]

Important: Price is at time 0, but the first dividend is at time 1. You always discount future dividends back to today. Why? Because you buy today, but you receive the first cash flow next year.

Single Growth Review (Gordon Model)

If dividends grow forever at a constant rate g, the infinite sum compresses to:

P₀ = D₁ / (r - g)
VERY IMPORTANT:
  • Price is at time 0
  • Dividend used is D₁ (the next dividend)

‚è±Ô∏è The Critical Time Rule (The Golden Rule)

The price at time t depends on the dividend at time t+1.

Pt = Dt+1 / (r - g)

The value of a stream of dividends is always placed ONE PERIOD BEFORE the first dividend.

If the first dividend is at time T, the value (Price) is at time T−1. Always.

THE TWO-STAGE MODEL

We split valuation into two distinct parts: Phase 1 (High growth) + Phase 2 (Stable growth).
Total Share Value = PV(Phase 1) + PV(Phase 2)

üöÄ PHASE 1: Supernormal Growth (Finite)

Dividends grow at g₁ for N years. We compute each dividend explicitly:

D₁
D₂ = D₁(1 + g₁)
...
DN

Discount each of them to today (Time 0):

PVphase1 = ∑ [ Dt / (1+r)ᵃ ]

‚öì PHASE 2: Stable Growth (Infinite)

Stable growth starts at year N+1 forever at rate g₂. We apply the model in 3 steps:

Step 1 ‚Äî Identify the first stable dividend
DN+1 = DN(1 + g₂)
Step 2 ‚Äî Apply Gordon at the correct time

Because the first stable dividend is at year N+1, the value of all stable dividends is located at year N.

PN = DN+1 / (r - g₂)
üß† WHY YEAR N? The value must be placed ONE YEAR BEFORE the first dividend.
Step 3 ‚Äî Discount back to today

The value PN is N years in the future. We must deflate it to get today's value:

PVphase2 = PN / (1+r)ᵃ

Final Share Value

P₀ = PVphase1 + PVphase2
