EX 2: TWO-STAGE GORDON MODEL

üí° Why Two Stages?

Real companies (like Tech startups) grow very fast at the beginning (g₁), but they cannot grow faster than the economy forever. Eventually, they slow down to a stable, long-term rate (g₂). To value them, we must separate these two lives.

The Core Principle of Share Valuation

A share is worth the present value of all future dividends. Price today reflects future cash flows.

P₀ = ∑ [ Dt / (1+r)ᵃ ]

Important: Price is at time 0, but the first dividend is at time 1. You always discount future dividends back to today.

Single Growth Review (Gordon Model)

If dividends grow forever at a constant rate g, the model simplifies to:

P₀ = D₁ / (r - g)
VERY IMPORTANT:
  • Price is at time 0
  • Dividend used is D₁ (the next dividend)

Why? Because you buy today, but the first cash flow is next year.

‚è±Ô∏è The Critical Time Rule (This is the key)

General Gordon formula: The price at time t depends on the dividend at time t+1.

Pt = Dt+1 / (r - g)

The value of a stream of dividends is always placed ONE PERIOD BEFORE the first dividend.

If the first dividend is at time T, the value applies to time T−1. Always.

THE TWO-STAGE MODEL STRUCTURE

We split valuation into two periods: Phase 1 (High growth) + Phase 2 (Stable growth).

üöÄ PHASE 1: Supernormal Growth (Finite)

Dividends grow at rate g₁ for N years. We compute each dividend explicitly:

D₁
D₂ = D₁(1 + g₁)
...
DN

Value at time 0:

PVPhase1 = ∑ [ Dt / (1+r)ᵃ ]

‚öì PHASE 2: Stable Growth (Infinite)

Stable growth starts at year N+1 forever at rate g₂. We apply the model in 3 steps:

Step 1 ‚Äî Identify the first stable dividend
DN+1 = DN(1 + g₂)
Step 2 ‚Äî Apply Gordon at the correct time

Because the first stable dividend is at year N+1, the value of all stable dividends is located at year N.

PN = DN+1 / (r - g₂)
üß† WHY YEAR N? Because N is the exact "contact point" between the high-growth phase and stability. Excel Implementation (Terminal Value P_N):
=(D_N * (1 + g2)) / (r - g2)
Step 3 ‚Äî Discount back to today

The value PN is N years in the future. To bring it back to today, we divide by (1+r)N:

PVPhase2 = PN / (1+r)N
Excel Implementation (Final PV of Phase 2):
=P_N / (1 + r)^N

Final Share Value

P₀ = PVPhase1 + PVPhase2
// Total Price in Excel
=PV_Phase1 + PV_Phase2
