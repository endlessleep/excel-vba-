DEEP DIVE: CASE 2 (RATE IS GIVEN)

The Problem: You know the Capital, the Rate, and the Duration, but you do not know the specific Payment.

// The Magic Excel Formula:
=PMT(interest_rate ; loan_term ; -loan_principal)
  • Detail 1: loan_term = the total number of periods.
  • Detail 2: IMPORTANT You must put a minus sign (-) in front of the principal.

What PMT actually does

PMT calculates the exact fixed payment required to totally amortize the loan at the end of every period, meaning the final remaining principal will be exactly 0.

ECONOMIC INTUITION

Explain that when you borrow, the bank gives you a Present Value (PV) today. In exchange, you promise future payments that must be discounted because of interest over time.

THE CORE CONDITION (Golden Rule)
The loan today MUST equal the Present Value of all future payments.

The Mathematics

Loan = Pmt/(1+r) + Pmt/(1+r)² + ... + Pmt/(1+r)ⁿ

PMT finds the exact value of the 'Payment' that solves this equation perfectly.

Note:

The payment is always calculated at the end of the period by default in Excel (Type = 0).
